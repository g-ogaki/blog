# Design Intent

monipy.org is a carefully crafted personal website that introduces its author through refined visual design and thoughtful interaction.

The homepage establishes personality and technical sophistication through restrained motion, generous whitespace, and a strong visual identity. As visitors move deeper into the site, the interface intentionally becomes quieter, allowing the writing itself to become the primary experience.

The goal is not to impress visitors with visual effects, but to communicate craftsmanship and encourage exploration.

---

# Core Principles

## Craftsmanship

Every visual element should feel intentional.

Quality is communicated through careful attention to typography, spacing, composition, imagery, and interaction rather than decoration.

The website should feel carefully crafted rather than expensive.

---

## Progressive Quietness

The website intentionally becomes quieter as visitors move deeper into the content.

### Home

Purpose:

Introduce the author.

Characteristics:

* Modern
* Refined
* Memorable
* Visually engaging
* Personality-driven

---

### Blog

Purpose:

Help readers discover content.

Characteristics:

* Clean
* Functional
* Easy to navigate
* Minimal distractions

---

### Article

Purpose:

Help readers think.

Characteristics:

* Reading-first
* Typography-focused
* Calm
* Minimal interface
* Content above decoration

---

# Design Personality

The website should feel:

* Professional
* Curious
* Modern
* Refined

The website should **not** feel:

* Corporate
* Cute
* Noisy
* Dashboard-like

---

# First Impression

A visitor opening the homepage should think:

* "This person has excellent taste."
* "I want to read more."

The homepage should communicate personality before functionality.

---

# Motion Philosophy

Animation exists to communicate quality.

Motion should be:

* subtle
* smooth
* purposeful

Motion should never become the primary attraction.

If an animation can be removed without reducing perceived quality, it probably should be removed.

---

# Visual Hierarchy

Priority:

1. Content
2. Typography
3. Whitespace
4. Imagery
5. Motion
6. Decoration

Decoration should never compete with content.

---

# Timelessness

Prefer timeless design over fashionable design.

Avoid visual trends that are likely to become dated quickly.

---

# Human Presence

The homepage should feel personal.

A portrait of the author is preferred over an illustration or abstract graphic.

The portrait should feel somewhere between professional and natural.

---

# Design Goal

The homepage should express the author.

The blog should organize ideas.

Articles should disappear behind the writing.
